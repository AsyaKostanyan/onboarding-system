The Accountability Framework

Note

Accountability is a defining feature of FPAS Mark II. It ensures that monetary policy decisions are transparent, disciplined, and aligned with the mandate of price stability.

The Accountability Framework provides structure for how the Central Bank of Armenia evaluates policy outcomes, reviews internal processes, and communicates the results to the public. Under FPAS Mark II, accountability is continuous, not episodic‚Äîit is built directly into the 29-day policy cycle and the Bank‚Äôs communication strategy.

1. Accountability and Credibility under FPAS Mark II

Accountability reinforces credibility by showing that policy decisions are evaluated against clear objectives, that mistakes are acknowledged and addressed, and that processes improve over time. Under FPAS Mark II, accountability is not a one-off exercise; it is embedded in every policy round.

The framework ensures that:

  • decisions are judged relative to the inflation target and broader mandate,
  • uncertainty is explicitly recognized through a least-regrets perspective, and
  • policy performance is reviewed consistently across time.

In this way, accountability becomes a core channel through which FPAS Mark II helps the Central Bank of Armenia build and maintain public trust.

2. Accountability as Culture, Not Just Procedure

Accountability in FPAS Mark II is not merely procedural‚Äîit is cultural. The framework promotes:

  • Humility in decision-making ‚Äî recognizing that uncertainty is unavoidable and that mistakes can occur.
  • Openness about uncertainty ‚Äî being clear about what is known and unknown, and how risks are being managed.
  • Willingness to revise assessments ‚Äî updating views and scenarios when new information arrives.
  • Clear communication of risks ‚Äî explaining the balance of risks and the least-regrets logic behind decisions.

When accountability is part of the culture, it becomes a source of strength rather than a constraint. It supports better decisions, stronger communication, and more effective policy transmission.
