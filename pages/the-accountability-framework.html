1. Accountability and Credibility under FPAS Mark II

Accountability reinforces credibility by showing that policy decisions are evaluated against clear objectives, that mistakes are acknowledged and addressed, and that processes improve over time. Under FPAS Mark II, accountability is not a one-off exercise; it is embedded in every policy round.

The framework ensures that:

  • decisions are judged relative to the inflation target and broader mandate,
  • uncertainty is explicitly recognized through a least-regrets perspective, and
  • policy performance is reviewed consistently across time.

In this way, accountability becomes a core channel through which FPAS Mark II helps the Central Bank of Armenia build and maintain public trust.

2. Assessing Policy Outcomes within an Uncertain World

Policy success is assessed in a world of uncertainty. Rather than rigid scorekeeping against a single forecast, FPAS Mark II emphasizes evaluating outcomes within a least-regrets, uncertainty-aware framework.

The Accountability Framework looks at:

  • Inflation relative to the target, including measures of underlying inflation that filter out temporary noise.
  • Real activity and labor market indicators, capturing economic slack and employment conditions.
  • Financial stability metrics, including credit growth, asset prices, and balance-sheet risks.
  • External and geopolitical risks, which can materially affect inflation and output paths.

By assessing policy outcomes across these dimensions, the Bank can judge whether its decisions were prudent and consistent with the least-regrets mindset.

3. Internal Accountability and Institutional Learning

Internal accountability is strengthened through a series of structured post-round reviews and diagnostics that are integrated into the 29-day policy cycle.

These include:

  • Forecast performance assessment ‚Äî comparing projections to outcomes and identifying systematic biases.
  • Scenario effectiveness evaluations ‚Äî examining whether the scenarios used for decision-making captured the most relevant risks.
  • Credibility and expectations analysis ‚Äî assessing how well inflation and policy expectations are anchored.
  • Communication quality checks ‚Äî reviewing whether internal and external messages were clear, coherent, and aligned with the framework.

These internal reviews build institutional memory and reinforce learning across policy rounds, making the framework stronger over time.

4. External Accountability and Democratic Legitimacy

Transparency also requires external accountability. Under FPAS Mark II, the Central Bank of Armenia provides the public and stakeholders with a comprehensive view of decisions, reasoning, and outcomes through:

  • The Monetary Policy Report, which presents forecasts, scenarios, and the analytical backbone of policy decisions.
  • The Executive Statement, summarizing the decision and its main justifications.
  • Attributed minutes, which reflect the range of views on the Board and how individual members interpreted the balance of risks.
  • Press conferences, where the Board and staff explain decisions and respond to questions.
  • Annual reviews of monetary policy performance and communication.

External accountability reinforces democratic legitimacy and allows experts, analysts, and the public to scrutinize policy decisions under FPAS Mark II.

5. Accountability as Culture, Not Just Procedure

Accountability in FPAS Mark II is not merely procedural‚Äîit is cultural. The framework promotes:

  • Humility in decision-making ‚Äî recognizing that uncertainty is unavoidable and that mistakes can occur.
  • Openness about uncertainty ‚Äî being clear about what is known and unknown, and how risks are being managed.
  • Willingness to revise assessments ‚Äî updating views and scenarios when new information arrives.
  • Clear communication of risks ‚Äî explaining the balance of risks and the least-regrets logic behind decisions.

When accountability is part of the culture, it becomes a source of strength rather than a constraint. It supports better decisions, stronger communication, and more effective policy transmission.

6. Conclusion: Accountability as a Pillar of FPAS Mark II

The Accountability Framework of FPAS Mark II ensures that the Central Bank of Armenia remains transparent, credible, and aligned with its mandate. By integrating accountability into analysis, communication, and institutional learning, the Bank strengthens public trust and enhances the effectiveness of monetary policy.
