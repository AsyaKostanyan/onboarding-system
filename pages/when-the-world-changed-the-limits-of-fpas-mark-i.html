When the World Changed: The Limits of FPAS Mark I

Note

FPAS Mark I was designed for a world that looked relatively stable ‚Äî with modest shocks, well-behaved financial markets, and a clear link between the policy rate, the real economy, and inflation. But over time, the global environment changed. Large commodity-price swings, financial crises, and persistent uncertainty revealed the limits of frameworks based on small, temporary shocks around a single baseline.

This lesson explains why FPAS Mark I became less reliable as a guide for policy ‚Äî and how those limitations motivated the transition to FPAS Mark II and the Prudent Risk Management Approach. The key insight is that modern central banks must think in terms of scenarios, risks, and least-regrets choices, not just baseline forecasts.
