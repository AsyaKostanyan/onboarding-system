Measuring and Enhancing Transparency

Note

In this session, we explore how FPAS Mark II strengthens transparency as a core element of credible monetary policy. Transparency under FPAS Mark II helps anchor expectations, reduce uncertainty, and support clearer policy transmission. It lowers the risk of misinterpretation and helps markets internalize the central bank‚Äôs risk assessment rather than relying on speculation.

Under FPAS Mark II, transparency is embedded throughout the framework: in scenario design, communication, minutes, forecasts, and post-round assessments. It is not cosmetic. It is foundational to how we explain our decisions and how we earn and maintain credibility over time.

1. Why Transparency Matters under FPAS Mark II

Transparency under FPAS Mark II plays a central role in anchoring expectations, reducing uncertainty, and strengthening policy transmission. By clearly explaining our framework and decisions, we lower the risk that markets misinterpret our actions and instead encourage them to internalize our risk assessment.

Rather than relying on speculation, well-designed transparency allows households, firms, and financial markets to understand:

  • how we see the balance of risks,
  • how uncertainty affects our decisions, and
  • how we are likely to respond as new information arrives.

FPAS Mark II integrates transparency into the entire policy cycle‚Äîforecasts, scenarios, meetings, communication, and post-round evaluations‚Äîso that credibility is built through openness, clarity, and disciplined reasoning.

2. Transparency Tools at the Central Bank of Armenia

The Central Bank of Armenia uses a rich set of tools to put these principles into practice. These include:

  • Transparency Report that summarize the discussion and perspectives.
  • Individual votes and justifications, showing how each member assessed the risks and trade-offs. It provides a concise explanation of the decision and its rationale.
  • The Monetary Policy Report, which presents forecasts, scenarios, and the analytical backbone of the decision.
  • Scenario-based communication that highlights alternative paths, risks, and trade-offs.
  • Press conferences and analyst briefings, where Board members and staff answer questions and clarify how uncertainty is affecting decisions.

These tools work together to clarify the Board‚Äôs reasoning under uncertainty and to show how FPAS Mark II is applied in practice.
