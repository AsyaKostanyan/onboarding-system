Decision-Making Transparency and Individual Accountability

Note

In this session, we explore how FPAS Mark II strengthens policy credibility through decision-making transparency and individual accountability. Decision-making transparency is a defining feature of FPAS Mark II. It shows how individual policymakers interpret risks, how they apply the least-regrets mindset, and how their votes shape policy outcomes.

For the Central Bank of Armenia, institutionalizing decision-making transparency and individual accountability is a major step toward world-class standards in monetary policy governance. Accountability extends beyond the institution to individual Board members, allowing the public to see who supported which decision, why they supported it, and how their reasoning fits within the FPAS Mark II framework.

1. Why Decision-Making Transparency Matters

Accountability under FPAS Mark II is not limited to the institution‚Äîit extends to individual Board members. This builds trust by allowing the public to see:

  • who supported which decision,
  • why they supported it, and
  • how their reasoning aligns with the FPAS Mark II framework.

Clear accountability incentivizes disciplined analysis, transparency in reasoning, and coherence in policy communication. It reinforces credibility by showing that decisions are made thoughtfully and consistently with the Bank‚Äôs mandate.

2. Disagreement as a Strength in FPAS Mark II

FPAS Mark II treats disagreement not as a weakness but as a strength. When Board members interpret risks differently, their perspectives help refine the institution‚Äôs understanding of uncertainty.

The framework encourages:

  • reasoned disagreement grounded in evidence and scenarios,
  • openness to alternative interpretations of the data,
  • clarification of asymmetric risks and tail events, and
  • discipline in explaining judgments in a coherent way.

Individual accountability allows the public to see these differences without undermining the unity of the institution‚Äôs final decision. The decision belongs to the Board as a whole, but the reasoning of individuals is transparent.

3. Timeliness and Structured Communication

FPAS Mark II also introduces strict timelines for publishing Transparency Report, attributed minutes, the Monetary Policy Report, and supporting analytical materials.

Timeliness reinforces credibility:

  • Delayed communication creates unnecessary uncertainty and opens space for speculation.
  • Structured timing ensures that the public receives information consistently and predictably.
  • Regular publication of decisions and reasoning supports more effective policy transmission.

Together, the timing and structure of communication make decision-making transparency an integral part of the FPAS Mark II policy cycle.
